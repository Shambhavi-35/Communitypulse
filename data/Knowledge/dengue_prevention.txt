TITLE: Dengue Prevention and Response Guidelines (Community Health)

Dengue is spread by Aedes mosquitoes that breed in stagnant water. Common early symptoms
include sudden high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain,
rash, and mild bleeding (nose or gums). Symptoms typically appear 4-10 days after a bite.

When to seek immediate care: persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain, bleeding, difficulty
breathing, or fever that suddenly drops after several days of high fever (warning sign of
plasma leakage). Patients with these warning signs should go to the nearest clinic or hospital
within a few hours, not wait for a scheduled appointment.

Household prevention: empty or cover all water-storage containers weekly, remove standing
water from flower pots, tires, and gutters, use mosquito nets and repellents, and keep windows
screened. Community health workers should inspect high-risk localities weekly during monsoon
season and report breeding sites.

Locality response protocol: if a locality reports more than 5 new dengue-like cases in a
7-day period, the public health team should trigger a fogging operation, door-to-door breeding
site inspection, and a public advisory within 48 hours.
