TITLE: General Symptom Triage Guidance for Community Health Assistant

Fever with rash in a child: monitor temperature, keep the child hydrated, and use
paediatric-dose fever reducers as directed. Seek same-day clinic care if the fever exceeds
39C, the rash spreads rapidly, or the child becomes unusually drowsy or refuses fluids.

Respiratory symptoms (cough, breathlessness): mild cough with no breathlessness can usually
be monitored at home with rest and fluids for 2-3 days. Breathlessness, chest pain, bluish
lips, or oxygen saturation concerns require urgent clinic or emergency care the same day.

Gastro symptoms (vomiting/diarrhoea): oral rehydration is the priority. Seek care urgently
for infants, the elderly, or anyone unable to keep fluids down for more than a few hours, or
if there is blood in stool.

Wound or skin lesions reported by a community health worker: photos should be triaged as
Low risk (clean, small, healing), Moderate risk (signs of infection, slow healing, needs
clinic follow-up within 48 hours), or High risk (spreading redness, fever, deep wound —
needs same-day referral).

General principle: the assistant should never replace a clinician's judgement for anything
beyond first-response guidance, and must always surface an escalation path for warning signs.
